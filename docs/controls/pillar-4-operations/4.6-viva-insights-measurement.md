# Control 4.6: Microsoft Viva Insights -- Copilot Impact Measurement

**Control ID:** 4.6
**Pillar:** Operations & Monitoring
**Regulatory Reference:** Internal Governance, SOX Section 404 (Control Effectiveness), FFIEC IT Examination Handbook
**Last Verified:** 2026-02-17
**Governance Levels:** Baseline / Recommended / Regulated

---

## Objective

Establish governance controls for the Copilot Impact Dashboard in Microsoft Viva Insights -- including productivity metrics, time savings analysis, adoption patterns, and ROI measurement -- while maintaining appropriate privacy safeguards for individual employee data and supporting compliance with internal governance requirements for AI technology investment decisions.

## Why This Matters for FSI

Financial institutions face increasing pressure from boards, regulators, and shareholders to demonstrate the value and responsible governance of AI technology investments. The Copilot Impact Dashboard in Viva Insights provides data-driven measurement of Copilot's effect on organizational productivity, but this measurement capability introduces governance considerations unique to regulated environments.

From a regulatory perspective, institutions that deploy AI tools are expected to monitor their effectiveness and impact. The FFIEC IT Examination Handbook expects institutions to evaluate whether technology investments deliver intended benefits and to adjust governance controls based on observed outcomes. SOX Section 404 requires that management's assessment of internal controls include consideration of how new technology affects control effectiveness.

From a privacy perspective, the Copilot Impact Dashboard aggregates behavioral data about how employees interact with AI tools. This data -- while designed to be anonymized and aggregated -- could, if improperly accessed or insufficiently aggregated, reveal individual work patterns that may implicate employee privacy protections.

From an investment governance perspective, financial institution boards and risk committees require evidence-based reporting on technology investments. The Copilot Impact Dashboard provides the data foundation for ROI measurement, but that measurement must be conducted with appropriate methodology, documented assumptions, and governance oversight.

## Control Description

**Copilot Impact Dashboard Overview**

The Copilot Impact Dashboard in Viva Insights provides the following measurement categories:

| Category | Metrics | Data Source |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| Adoption | Active users, feature usage frequency, adoption curve | M365 usage telemetry |
| Productivity | Time saved in meetings, email, document creation | Viva Insights behavioral signals |
| Assisted Hours | Estimated hours of AI-assisted work per user per week | Copilot interaction telemetry |
| Sentiment | User satisfaction with Copilot (survey-based) | Viva Insights sentiment surveys |
| Quality | Meeting effectiveness, communication quality | Viva Insights work pattern analysis |
| ROI | Estimated dollar value of time savings | Calculated from time metrics and cost assumptions |

**Privacy Safeguards for Individual Data**

The Copilot Impact Dashboard is designed with privacy protections, but financial institutions should verify and enhance these controls:

| Safeguard | Description | FSI Configuration |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Aggregation Minimum | Data only shown when group size meets minimum | Set to 25+ for regulated departments |
| De-identification | Individual user data not displayed in dashboard | Verify with Viva Insights admin settings |
| Opt-Out | Employees can opt out of Insights data collection | Document and communicate opt-out process |
| Data Retention | Impact data retained for configurable period | Align with organizational data retention policy |
| Access Control | Dashboard access limited to designated roles | Restrict to Viva Insights Analyst and approved leaders |
| Attribute Filtering | Limit which organizational attributes are available | Restrict to department and location; exclude sensitive attributes |

**Productivity Metrics Governance**

Productivity metrics require governance because they can be misused:

- **Time Savings:** Estimated time saved should be understood as approximations, not precise measurements. Methodology and assumptions should be documented.
- **Meeting Efficiency:** Metrics on meeting duration reduction should not be used to penalize teams with longer meetings, which may reflect legitimate business complexity.
- **Communication Patterns:** Email and chat volume reductions should be evaluated in context. Reduced communication volume is not inherently positive -- it could indicate disengagement.
- **Focus Time:** Increases in focus time should be validated against actual work output, not assumed to equal productivity improvement.

**Adoption Pattern Analysis**

The dashboard provides adoption pattern insights:

- **Department Comparison:** Usage rates across departments (respecting aggregation minimums)
- **Feature Adoption:** Which Copilot features are most and least used
- **Adoption Velocity:** How quickly new users become regular users
- **Drop-off Analysis:** Identification of users who try Copilot but stop using it
- **Use Case Distribution:** Which tasks users most frequently use Copilot for

**ROI Measurement Framework**

ROI measurement for Copilot should follow a structured methodology:

| Component | Measurement Approach | Governance Requirement |
|-----------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Time Savings | Hours saved per user per week (Viva Insights estimate) | Document estimation methodology |
| Cost Avoidance | Reduced need for specific manual processes | Validate with business unit leaders |
| Quality Improvement | Error reduction, consistency improvement | Measure through existing quality programs |
| License Cost | Per-user Copilot license cost | Source from procurement records |
| Implementation Cost | Training, change management, governance overhead | Track through project management |
| Net ROI | (Benefits - Costs) / Costs | Present with confidence intervals |

## Governance Levels

### Baseline

- [ ] Enable the Copilot Impact Dashboard in Viva Insights for designated administrators
- [ ] Configure aggregation minimums appropriate for the organization (minimum 25 for regulated firms)
- [ ] Document who has access to the Impact Dashboard and their business justification
- [ ] Establish a quarterly review cadence for Copilot impact metrics
- [ ] Communicate to employees how productivity data is collected and used
- [ ] Document the methodology and assumptions underlying ROI calculations

### Recommended

- [ ] Create a standardized Copilot ROI report for board/committee consumption
- [ ] Implement department-level impact analysis with appropriate aggregation protections
- [ ] Establish baseline measurements before Copilot rollout for before/after comparison
- [ ] Conduct sentiment surveys to complement quantitative metrics with qualitative feedback
- [ ] Integrate impact data with the firm's technology investment governance process
- [ ] Compare impact metrics against pre-deployment business case projections
- [ ] Review and adjust governance controls based on observed impact patterns

### Regulated

- [ ] Include Copilot impact measurement in the annual technology risk assessment
- [ ] Present impact findings to the board risk committee or technology committee quarterly
- [ ] Conduct independent validation of ROI methodology and calculations annually
- [ ] Maintain audit trail of all access to the Copilot Impact Dashboard
- [ ] Include impact measurement governance in SOX ITGC scope where applicable
- [ ] Document all management decisions made based on Copilot impact data
- [ ] Implement controls to prevent impact data from being used in individual performance evaluations

## Setup & Configuration

### Step 1: Enable Copilot Impact Dashboard

1. Navigate to **Viva Insights > Copilot Dashboard** (accessible to Viva Insights Analyst role)
2. Verify that data population requirements are met:
   - Sufficient Copilot-licensed users
   - Viva Insights data processing enabled
   - Minimum aggregation thresholds met
3. Configure dashboard access:
   - Assign Viva Insights Analyst role to designated governance personnel
   - Restrict access to only those with documented business justification

### Step 2: Configure Privacy Settings

1. Set aggregation minimum:
   - Navigate to Viva Insights admin settings
   - Set minimum group size to 25 (or higher for sensitive departments)
2. Configure data exclusions:
   - Exclude specific departments from impact measurement if appropriate
   - Document exclusion rationale
3. Verify de-identification:
   - Confirm that no individual user data is visible in dashboard views
   - Test with various filter combinations to verify aggregation holds

### Step 3: Establish Measurement Baseline

Before or immediately after Copilot deployment:

1. Capture baseline productivity metrics:
   - Average meeting hours per user per week
   - Average email processing time
   - Average document creation time
   - Current collaboration patterns
2. Document baseline data sources and collection dates
3. Define target improvement metrics and timeframes

### Step 4: Create Reporting Templates

Establish standardized reporting templates for different audiences:

| Audience | Report Content | Frequency |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| Board Risk Committee | High-level ROI, adoption trends, risk indicators | Quarterly |
| Executive Leadership | Department comparison, feature adoption, ROI progress | Monthly |
| IT Governance | Technical usage, anomaly detection, capacity planning | Monthly |
| Compliance | Usage in regulated activities, supervisory coverage adequacy | Quarterly |
| Finance | License utilization, cost allocation, ROI validation | Quarterly |

### Step 5: Configure Automated Reporting

1. Schedule automated dashboard exports for governance distribution
2. Configure alerts for significant metric changes:
   - Adoption drop >20% in any department
   - ROI metrics falling below business case projections
   - Anomalous usage patterns warranting investigation

## Financial Sector Considerations

**Board-Level Reporting:** Financial institution boards are expected to exercise oversight of significant technology investments. Copilot impact reporting provides the data foundation for this oversight. Reports should be structured to show both benefits achieved and risks identified, not just positive metrics. Present ROI with appropriate caveats about estimation methodology.

**Investment Governance:** Financial institutions with formal technology investment governance processes should integrate Copilot impact measurement into those processes. If the institution made a business case for Copilot adoption, impact metrics should be compared against business case projections with variance analysis.

**Regulatory Examination Response:** If regulators ask about the institution's AI governance program, impact measurement demonstrates mature governance. However, be prepared to explain the limitations of productivity metrics and how the institution prevents their misuse in employment decisions.

**Privacy Regulatory Risk:** Some jurisdictions have employee monitoring regulations that may apply to Viva Insights data collection. Consult employment counsel regarding notification requirements, especially in jurisdictions with specific AI transparency laws.

**Auditor Expectations:** External auditors assessing IT general controls may request evidence of technology monitoring. Copilot impact data, when combined with usage analytics (Control 4.5) and audit logging (Pillar 3), provides a comprehensive evidence package.

**Methodology Documentation:** For SOX-reporting entities, any financial metrics derived from Copilot impact data (e.g., cost savings used in financial projections) should have documented calculation methodologies subject to management review.
