    Control 2.11: Copilot Pages Security and Sharing Controls

    Control ID: 2.11 Pillar: Security & Protection Regulatory Reference: GLBA 501(b), FINRA 4511 Last Verified: 2026-02-17 Governance Levels: Baseline / Recommended / Regulated

    Objective

    Establish security and sharing controls for Copilot Pages — a collaboration surface where Copilot-generated content can be persisted, shared, and co-edited. Because Copilot Pages may contain aggregated content from multiple sources (including sensitive financial data), they require specific governance around creation permissions, sharing scope, sensitivity label inheritance, retention policies, and external access restrictions. This control supports compliance with GLBA safeguard requirements and FINRA record-keeping obligations.

    Why This Matters for FSI

    • GLBA 501(b) requires safeguards for customer information — Copilot Pages may contain summaries of customer data aggregated from multiple sources, creating a new data surface that must be governed
    • FINRA Rule 4511 requires firms to make and preserve books and records — Copilot Pages that contain business communications, client-related summaries, or investment recommendations may constitute firm records requiring retention
    • FINRA Rule 2210 (Communications with the Public) may apply if Copilot Pages contain content that could be shared externally with clients — the firm must supervise such communications
    • SEC 17a-3/4 specifies record-keeping requirements — Copilot Pages that document advisory activities, recommendations, or client interactions may fall under these requirements
    • SOX Section 404 internal controls should address new collaboration surfaces — Pages that aggregate financial data must be subject to appropriate access controls

    Control Description

    Copilot Pages is a new collaboration surface in Microsoft 365 that allows users to persist, edit, and share content generated by Copilot. Pages are stored in the user's OneDrive and can be shared with specific people or groups.

    Copilot Pages Data Flow

    User interacts with Copilot
         │
         ▼
  Copilot generates response

  User selects "Create Page"

  │  ├─ Stored in OneDrive  │

    Private        Shared

           users         (if allowed)

    Pages Security Controls

    Control Area Description Configuration Location
    Creation permissions Who can create Copilot Pages M365 Admin Center > Copilot settings
    Sharing scope Who Pages can be shared with SharePoint/OneDrive sharing settings
    Sensitivity labels Label inheritance from source content Microsoft Purview > Information Protection
    External sharing Whether Pages can be shared externally SharePoint Admin Center > Sharing
    Retention How long Pages are retained Microsoft Purview > Data Lifecycle Management
    DLP DLP policies applied to Pages content Microsoft Purview > DLP
    eDiscovery Pages discoverable and holdable Microsoft Purview > eDiscovery

    Sensitivity Label Inheritance for Pages

    Source Content Labels Resulting Page Label Behavior
    All sources unlabeled Tenant default label (e.g., Internal) Default label applied
    Mixed: Internal + Confidential Confidential (highest label) Most restrictive label wins
    Any source: Highly Confidential Page creation may be blocked DLP policy prevents Page creation from HC content
    Single source: Confidential Confidential Label inherited directly

    Pages Sharing Permissions Model

    Sharing Level Description Default Recommendation
    Only me Creator-only access Default for new Pages
    Specific people Named internal users Allowed with label restrictions
    People with the link Anyone with the link in the org Restrict to Baseline level only
    External guests Users outside the organization Disabled for FSI
    Anyone Anonymous access Disabled — never appropriate for FSI

    Pages Retention Considerations

    Content Type Retention Requirement Rationale
    Client-related summaries 6 years (broker-dealer) or 5 years (RIA) SEC 17a-4 / Advisers Act 204-2
    Investment recommendations 6 years (broker-dealer) SEC 17a-4
    General business content 3 years minimum Firm policy
    Draft content (never shared) 1 year minimum Firm policy
    M&A deal-related content 7 years SOX + litigation hold

    Word No No N/A N/A Word Copilot does not create Pages
    Excel No No N/A N/A Excel Copilot does not create Pages
    PowerPoint No No N/A N/A PowerPoint Copilot does not create Pages
    Outlook No No N/A N/A Outlook Copilot does not create Pages
    Teams Yes Yes Yes Yes Teams Copilot can create Pages from chat
    OneNote No No N/A N/A OneNote Copilot does not create Pages
    Loop Related Related Yes Yes Loop and Pages share infrastructure
    Copilot Pages Yes Yes Yes Yes Direct Pages interface
    SharePoint (Agents) Yes Yes Yes Yes Agent responses can create Pages

    Governance Levels

    Level Requirement Rationale
    Baseline Enable Pages with "Specific people" sharing only; disable external sharing for Pages; apply tenant default sensitivity label; enable DLP policies for Pages content; include Pages in retention policies Minimum governance — allows Pages functionality with basic protections suitable for controlled Copilot rollout
    Recommended All Baseline requirements plus: enforce sensitivity label inheritance from source content; configure label-based sharing restrictions (Confidential Pages cannot be shared broadly); create DLP policies specific to Pages with FSI SITs; enable eDiscovery for Pages; quarterly review of Pages sharing activity; user training on Pages data handling Comprehensive Pages governance that balances collaboration with data protection — suitable for most FSI firms
    Regulated All Recommended requirements plus: restrict Pages creation to approved user groups; mandatory sensitivity labeling for all Pages; Pages included in communication compliance policies; automated monitoring of Pages sharing patterns; Pages explicitly covered in the firm's records management policy; annual audit of Pages security configuration Maximum Pages governance for firms where every collaboration surface must be fully governed and auditable

    Setup & Configuration

    Step 1: Configure Pages Access

    Portal: Microsoft 365 Admin Center > Settings > Copilot > Pages

    1. Determine whether Pages should be available to all Copilot users or restricted to specific groups
    2. For Regulated environments, create a security group for Pages-eligible users
    3. Configure Pages access scope

    Step 2: Configure Sharing Settings

    Portal: SharePoint Admin Center > Policies > Sharing

    1. Copilot Pages inherit OneDrive sharing settings
    2. Set OneDrive external sharing to "Only people in your organization"
    3. Configure default sharing link type to "Specific people"
    4. Disable "Anyone" links for OneDrive
    # Connect to SharePoint Online Management Shell
Connect-SPOService -Url "https://contoso-admin.sharepoint.com"

# Configure OneDrive sharing (affects Pages)
Set-SPOTenant -OneDriveSharingCapability ExistingExternalUserSharingOnly
# Or for maximum restriction:
Set-SPOTenant -OneDriveSharingCapability Disabled

# Set default sharing link type
Set-SPOTenant -DefaultSharingLinkType Direct  # "Specific people"

    Step 3: Configure Sensitivity Label Inheritance

    1. Verify that sensitivity label policies are published to all Copilot users (see Control 2.2)
    2. Confirm label inheritance is enabled for Copilot-generated content
    3. Set default label for Pages when no source label exists

    Step 4: Apply DLP Policies to Pages

    Portal: Microsoft Purview > DLP > Policies

    1. Verify existing DLP policies cover OneDrive locations (Pages are stored in OneDrive)
    2. Create Pages-specific DLP rules if needed:
    3. Detect FSI SITs in Pages content
    4. Block sharing of Pages containing Confidential or higher labeled content with broad audiences

    Step 5: Configure Retention for Pages

    Portal: Microsoft Purview > Data Lifecycle Management > Retention policies

    1. Create or update retention policy to include OneDrive locations
    2. Set retention period based on content type and regulatory requirements
    3. Consider auto-applying retention labels based on content sensitivity

    Step 6: Enable eDiscovery for Pages

    1. Verify Pages content is searchable in eDiscovery (content search and eDiscovery cases)
    2. Confirm Pages can be placed on legal hold
    3. Test eDiscovery search for Pages content to validate retrieval

    Step 7: Monitor Pages Activity

    # Search audit logs for Pages-related activities
Search-UnifiedAuditLog -StartDate (Get-Date).AddDays(-30) -EndDate (Get-Date) `
  -Operations "PageCreated","PageShared","PageViewed" -ResultSize 5000

# Monitor Pages sharing patterns
Search-UnifiedAuditLog -StartDate (Get-Date).AddDays(-7) -EndDate (Get-Date) `
  -Operations "SharingSet" -ResultSize 5000 |
  Where-Object { $_.AuditData -match "Page" }

    Financial Sector Considerations

    • Record-Keeping Classification: Financial firms must determine whether Copilot Pages constitute "business records" under SEC 17a-3/4 and FINRA 4511. If a registered representative uses Copilot to generate a client meeting summary and saves it as a Page, this may be a firm record requiring preservation. Establish clear guidance for when Pages become records.
    • Client Communication Risk: If Copilot Pages are shared externally (which should be restricted), they could be classified as "communications with the public" under FINRA Rule 2210, requiring pre-use review and approval. Disable external sharing for Pages in FSI environments.
    • Aggregation Risk: Pages may aggregate content from multiple sources into a single document. A Page created from a Copilot Chat session could contain client data from multiple accounts, investment recommendations from different analysts, and market data — all in one shareable document. This aggregation creates data sensitivity that may exceed any individual source.
    • Supervision Integration: For broker-dealers, consider whether Pages should be included in communication compliance policies (see Pillar 3 controls). Pages containing investment recommendations or client-related content may require supervisory review.
    • Departing Employee Procedures: When employees depart, their Copilot Pages (stored in their OneDrive) should be included in the firm's offboarding data preservation procedures. Ensure Pages are captured before OneDrive access is revoked.
    • Litigation Hold: Copilot Pages may contain relevant content for litigation, regulatory investigations, or arbitration proceedings. Confirm that legal hold can be placed on Pages and that held content is preserved even if the user attempts to delete it.
    • Training: Users should be trained to understand that Pages are subject to the same governance as other firm documents — they are not "scratch pads" that exist outside the firm's data governance framework.

    Verification Criteria

    1. Pages Access Configuration: Verify Pages is enabled for the intended user population and restricted for others (if applicable)
    2. External Sharing Disabled: Confirm that Pages cannot be shared with external users — attempt to share a Page externally and verify it is blocked
    3. Sharing Scope: Create a Page and verify the default sharing link type is "Specific people" (not "Anyone" or "People in your organization")
    4. Label Inheritance: Create a Page from Copilot content that referenced a Confidential document — verify the Page inherits the Confidential label
    5. DLP Enforcement: Create a Page containing test sensitive data (e.g., test SSN) — verify the DLP policy detects and acts on the content
    6. Retention Policy: Verify Pages are included in the firm's retention policy by checking retention label application or policy scope
    7. eDiscovery Search: Run an eDiscovery content search targeting Pages — verify Pages content is discoverable
    8. Legal Hold: Place a test user on legal hold and verify their Pages content is preserved
    9. Audit Logging: Verify Pages creation, sharing, and viewing events appear in the Unified Audit Log
    10. User Training: Confirm that Copilot user training materials include guidance on Pages data handling and record-keeping responsibilities
