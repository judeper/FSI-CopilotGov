    **Control 3.8 updates (P3-07):**
    - Add documentation of **OCC Bulletin 2025-26** MRM proportionality guidance for community banks:
      - The OCC issued updated model risk management guidance acknowledging that community banks and smaller institutions require proportional MRM frameworks. The bulletin clarifies that the depth and complexity of model risk management should be commensurate with the institution's size, complexity, and risk profile.
      - For M365 Copilot governance: community banks deploying Copilot can implement a simplified MRM approach — model inventory registration, basic output monitoring, and vendor due diligence review — without the full validation infrastructure expected of large complex banking organizations.
      - Cite OCC Bulletin 2025-26 specifically as the source of proportionality guidance.

    - **Clarify Copilot's model status under SR 11-7:**
      - Strengthen the existing framing: M365 Copilot is a vendor-provided general-purpose LLM, not a bank-developed quantitative model. Under SR 11-7's definition, a "model" is "a quantitative method, system, or approach that applies statistical, economic, financial, or mathematical theories, techniques, and assumptions to process input data into quantitative estimates." Copilot straddles this definition — it processes input and produces output, but it is not a traditional quantitative model.
      - Document the practical resolution: most institutions register Copilot in their model inventory as a "Tier 3" or "limited-scope" model, focusing governance on usage controls, output monitoring, and vendor due diligence rather than traditional model validation.
      - Update tier recommendations: Baseline: model inventory entry with usage scope documentation; Recommended: model inventory + periodic output quality monitoring + vendor due diligence review; Regulated: full MRM lifecycle including annual validation report (focused on usage controls and output quality, not internal model architecture).

    **Control 3.10 updates (P3-08):**
    - Add **Reg S-P amendment details** (SEC adopted December 2023, effective dates staggered):
      - **72-hour vendor notification requirement**: SEC Rule 248.30(a)(3) now requires covered institutions to notify service providers of unauthorized access to customer information within 72 hours of detection. For M365 Copilot, this means incidents where Copilot improperly surfaces customer NPI must trigger the 72-hour notification chain to Microsoft as the service provider.
      - **Large entity compliance date**: June 2025 for larger covered entities (those filing reports under Exchange Act Section 13 or 15(d) with $1B+ in assets or AUM).
      - **Small entity compliance date**: December 2025 for smaller covered entities.
      - **Mandatory incident response programs**: SEC Rule 248.30(a)(4) requires written policies and procedures for an incident response program addressing unauthorized access to or use of customer information. The program must include procedures for assessing the nature and scope of incidents, containing and remediating incidents, and notifying affected individuals.

    - Connect to Copilot: document that Copilot-related incidents (e.g., Copilot surfacing NPI to unauthorized users due to oversharing, Copilot-generated content containing customer NPI sent to wrong recipients) constitute incidents under the Reg S-P incident response program.
    - Update tier recommendations with Reg S-P amendment requirements at each tier.
    - Cite 17 CFR Section 248.30(a)(3) and (a)(4) specifically.

    **All 3.8 and 3.10 playbooks:**
    - 3.8 portal-walkthrough: Add guidance for model inventory registration path. No new portal features expected.
    - 3.8 powershell-setup: Review for accuracy; no expected changes.
    - 3.8 verification-testing: Add test case for verifying model inventory entry exists for Copilot and includes proportionality classification.

    Per locked decision: when multiple related regulations appear in one control (17a-4 + off-channel fines + mobile recordkeeping), use unified narrative threading rather than separate sub-sections.

    - Add documentation of the **SEC 17a-4 audit-trail alternative to WORM**:
      - SEC Rule 17a-4(f)(2)(ii)(A) provides an alternative to traditional WORM storage: an "audit-trail alternative" where electronic records need not be stored in non-rewriteable format if the broker-dealer maintains an audit trail of all modifications, deletions, and access events for the record throughout its retention period.
      - For M365 Copilot content: Microsoft Purview retention labels with "regulatory record" declaration combined with Preservation Lock and the Purview audit trail may satisfy the audit-trail alternative, providing an alternative path for firms that cannot or choose not to implement full third-party WORM archival.
      - Add tier guidance: Baseline: standard Purview retention; Recommended: Purview retention with Preservation Lock; Regulated: third-party WORM archival OR audit-trail alternative with documented compliance justification.
      - Cite SEC Rule 17a-4(f)(2)(ii)(A) specifically.

    - Add **off-channel communications enforcement context** woven into the existing recordkeeping narrative:
      - Since 2021, the SEC and CFTC have imposed over $2 billion in fines on financial institutions for failure to preserve business communications conducted on unapproved channels (personal devices, messaging apps, non-firm email). This enforcement wave — sometimes called the "off-channel" or "WhatsApp" enforcement — underscores the regulatory imperative to capture ALL business communications, regardless of channel.
      - Connect to Copilot: M365 Copilot interactions conducted through approved Microsoft 365 channels (Teams, Outlook, SharePoint) are captured by Purview retention and audit infrastructure. However, if users access Copilot through mobile devices, web browsers outside managed environments, or third-party integrations, those interactions may fall outside the firm's recordkeeping infrastructure.
      - Frame as risk mitigation: deploying Copilot within M365 with proper retention policies actually helps mitigate off-channel risk by keeping AI-assisted interactions within the governed communication perimeter.

    - Add **mobile Copilot recordkeeping** considerations:
      - Copilot is accessible via mobile Microsoft 365 apps (Teams, Outlook, Word mobile). Mobile interactions generate the same audit events and are covered by the same retention policies — provided the mobile device is managed (Intune MDM/MAM) and uses the official Microsoft 365 apps.
      - Risk: if users access Copilot via unmanaged mobile browsers, interactions may not be captured by the firm's retention infrastructure.
      - Add Conditional Access tie-in: require managed device or compliant app for Copilot access (cross-reference to control 2.3 Conditional Access policies).
      - Add to tier table: Baseline: mobile Copilot via managed apps only; Recommended: Conditional Access blocking unmanaged browser access; Regulated: MDM-enforced device compliance + app protection policies + periodic mobile audit.

    - Thread all three topics (17a-4 alternative, off-channel enforcement, mobile recordkeeping) into a unified narrative that flows from the regulatory requirement to the enforcement context to the mobile-specific risk. Do NOT create three separate subsections — weave them together as a cohesive recordkeeping modernization discussion.
